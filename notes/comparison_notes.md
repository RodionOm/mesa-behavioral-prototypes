# Comparison: Survival vs Information Models

## Shared Structure

Both models use:

- Action abstraction
- Softmax policy
- Agent decision loop
- Regime switching

---

## Key Differences

| Aspect | Survival | Information |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Environment | Spatial | Social |
| Pressure | Resource + danger | Social influence |
| Dynamics | Cyclic | Convergent |
| Diversity | High | Lower |

---

## Insight

The framework generalizes structure, but not outcomes.

---

## Main Finding

> Behavior abstraction is reusable, but behavior itself is domain-dependent.

---

## Implication for Mesa

Mesa lacks:

- standardized behavior layer
- reusable decision pipelines
- unified diagnostics

---

## Conclusion

This supports adding:

- policy interfaces
- action systems
- behavioral tooling

to Mesa core or extensions.
